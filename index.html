⚡ Quick Presets
🎯 Keep Quality on TikTok, YouTube & Instagram
📐
Use the right resolution & bitrate

TikTok and YouTube both re-compress your upload. To survive their compression and still look great, you need to upload at higher quality than you want to end up with.

50 MbpsSet bitrate to 50 Mbps before uploading. The platform will compress it down, but starting higher means less detail lost.

TikTok: 1080×1920 YouTube: 1920×1080 Reels: 1080×1920

🔪
Always sharpen before uploading

Every platform applies a blur pass when compressing. Pre-sharpening your video compensates for this — the sharpened version after their compression will look as crisp as your original.

Use the 🔪 Sharpen chip for this. It runs a real unsharp mask GPU shader — not a fake contrast boost. Apply it even if your video looks sharp already.

Critical for TikTok Recommended for YT

🎨
Boost contrast & saturation slightly

Platform compression flattens contrast and mutes colours. Boosting them slightly before upload means they survive compression looking natural — not washed out.

Add +10–20 Contrast and +10–15 Saturation in Adjustments. Don't go too far — you want it to look right after their compression, not before.

Use "TikTok Ready" or "YouTube Ready" preset →

💎
4K upload = 1080p that actually looks 4K

YouTube gives 4K uploads a higher quality codec (VP9/AV1) even when displayed at 1080p. So uploading at 4K — even from a 1080p source — can make your 1080p stream look noticeably sharper.

Use the 💎 4K Ultra Crisp preset. SmartVideo upscales your video to 4K with sharpening applied, then you upload that to YouTube for a cleaner 1080p stream.

YouTube 4K trick

⏱️
Frame rate matters

Upload at the native frame rate of your footage. TikTok handles 30fps best. YouTube handles both 30fps and 60fps well — use 60fps for gaming or action content.

Avoid converting 24fps → 30fps or vice versa. It causes judder. If your original is 24fps cinematic footage, keep it 24fps.

TikTok: 30fps ideal YT: 30fps or 60fps

📦
MP4 H.264 is the safest format

Always upload MP4 — not WebM, MOV, or AVI. Every platform's ingest pipeline is optimised for H.264 MP4. Other formats get transcoded an extra time, losing quality.

SmartVideo exports MP4 by default. Keep it that way for uploads. WebM VP9 is great for embedding on websites, but not for social media uploads.
